## 🎯 Project Overview

This repository contains my complete, end-to-end data analytics capstone project completed during my internship at Apex Planet. The objective was to analyze the E-Commerce Dataset (100,000+ orders across 9 relational tables) to identify logistical bottlenecks, optimize payment strategies, and deliver actionable business intelligence to executive stakeholders.

Over the course of this project, I transformed raw, messy CSV files into a fully interactive Power BI dashboard, backed by strict statistical hypothesis testing.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Skills Demonstrated

* Data Engineering & Cleaning: Python, Pandas, Jupyter Notebook

* Database Management & EDA: SQL (SQLite via Python), Relational Data Modeling

* Business Intelligence & UI/UX: Microsoft Power BI, DAX, Custom AI UI Wireframing

* Statistical Analysis: Python (scipy.stats), A/B Hypothesis Testing (Two-Sample T-Tests)

* Data Storytelling: Executive Slide Deck Presentation, Video Walkthroughs

## 📂 Project Lifecycle & Deliverables

Phase 1: Data Immersion & Wrangling (Python)

* Objective: Clean and standardize 9 disjointed relational datasets.

* Key Achievements: Handled missing values without losing revenue data, standardized geographic text strings, and deduplicated records using complex composite keys (order_id + order_item_id).

Phase 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (SQL)

* Objective: Act as a Business Analyst to extract key KPIs.

* Key Achievements: Built a local SQLite database in Python. Executed complex multi-table JOIN operations and aggregations to identify top-performing categories, monthly acquisition trends, and logistics delays.

Phase 3: Interactive Dashboarding (Power BI)

* Objective: Build a scalable, executive-facing Business Intelligence tool.

* Key Achievements: Constructed a Star Schema relational data model in Power BI. Designed a premium, custom "Dark Mode" UI using AI-generated wireframes to track R$13.59M in revenue and isolate an 8% late delivery rate.

Phase 4: Data Storytelling & Statistical Validation

* Objective: Prove business theories using rigorous mathematical testing.

* Key Achievements: Ran Two-Sample Independent T-Tests using Python's SciPy library.

* Proven Hypothesis 1: Late deliveries cause a statistically significant drop in review scores (4.21 ⭐ down to 2.57 ⭐).

* Proven Hypothesis 2: Credit card users drive a statistically significant higher Average Order Value (AOV) than Boleto users.

## 💡 Key Business Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive data analysis, I pitched the following strategic recommendations to the executive team:

* Audit Regional Freight Carriers: The data isolated a 7.87% late delivery rate primarily clustered in the Southeast. Carriers failing SLAs must be penalized or replaced to protect brand retention.

* Delist High-Friction Items: A targeted SQL/Power BI matrix identified the exact Product IDs driving the highest volume of 1-star reviews due to quality or description mismatches.

* Incentivize Credit Card Checkout: Because T-Tests proved Credit Cards drive a higher AOV (R$ 163 vs R$ 145), targeted checkout discounts should be implemented to convert cash-buyers to credit.
